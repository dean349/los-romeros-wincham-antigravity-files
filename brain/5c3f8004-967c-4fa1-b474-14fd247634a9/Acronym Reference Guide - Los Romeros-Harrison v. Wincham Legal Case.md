## SECTION 1 — Spanish Tax & Regulatory Acronyms

| # | Acronym | Full Name | Description | Case Relevance |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **AEAT** | *Agencia Estatal de Administración Tributaria* | Spain's national tax authority — the equivalent of HMRC in Spain. Responsible for collecting taxes, processing refunds, and enforcing tax compliance for residents and non-residents alike. | The AEAT withheld 3% of the €315,000 sale proceeds (€9,450) via Modelo 211. The refund of any overpayment must be reclaimed from AEAT via Modelo 210. AEAT has formally ruled that the "Wincham Scheme" (UK Ltd holding Spanish property) can be "looked through." |
| 2 | **DGT** | *Dirección General de Tributos* | Spain's General Directorate of Taxes — the official tax policy body within the Ministry of Finance. Issues binding tax consultations (consultas vinculantes) that define how Spanish tax law applies in specific scenarios. | The DGT issued binding consultations confirming Spanish authorities can pierce the corporate veil of UK shell companies holding Spanish property, directly undermining Wincham's scheme. |
| 3 | **IRNR** | *Impuesto sobre la Renta de No Residentes* | Spain's Non-Resident Income Tax. Applies to income and capital gains earned in Spain by entities or individuals not resident in Spain. | The 19% IRNR rate applies to Los Romeros Limited's gain on selling the Lanzarote villa. The Modelo 210 is the tax return through which this is declared and settled. |
| 4 | **ISD** | *Impuesto sobre Sucesiones y Donaciones* | Spain's Inheritance and Gift Tax. Historically imposed at very high rates (up to 81%) on non-residents inheriting Spanish property. | The entire "Wincham Scheme" was sold to Phil Harrison's family as a way to route Spanish property through a UK company so that heirs would inherit UK *shares* (subject to UK IHT) rather than Spanish bricks (subject to punishing ISD rates). AEAT has ruled this does not achieve its intended effect. |
| 5 | **ITP** | *Impuesto de Transmisiones Patrimoniales* | Spanish Transfer Tax — equivalent to Stamp Duty Land Tax in the UK. Paid by the buyer when purchasing a second-hand (resale) property in Spain. Some Canary Island transactions use IGIC instead. | The original ITP paid when the property was first purchased is an allowable deduction when calculating the taxable gain for the Modelo 210 return — reducing the CGT payable or refund available. |
| 6 | **IGIC** | *Impuesto General Indirecto Canario* | The Canary Islands' own indirect tax — analogous to VAT on the Spanish mainland but unique to the autonomous Canary Islands region (including Lanzarote). Applies to new-build property rather than resale transactions. | Relevant when calculating allowable acquisition deductions on the Modelo 210 for the Lanzarote property. Whether ITP or IGIC applied depends on whether the property was purchased as new-build or resale. |
| 7 | **IIVTNU** | *Impuesto sobre el Incremento del Valor de los Terrenos de Naturaleza Urbana* | Plusvalía Municipal — the local Spanish tax on the increase in the cadastral value of urban land during the period of ownership. Levied by the local Ayuntamiento (municipality), not the national government. | Must be paid to the Tías Ayuntamiento within 30 days of completion (escritura). After the 2021 Spanish Constitutional Court ruling, Plusvalía is only payable where an actual gain on the land component occurred. |
| 8 | **CGT** | Capital Gains Tax | Tax levied on the profit made when disposing of an asset that has increased in value. Applies in both Spain (as IRNR) and the UK (as UK CGT) — creating potential double taxation on the same gain, mitigated via FTCR. | Phil Harrison faces CGT in both jurisdictions: Spanish CGT at 19% via Modelo 210, and UK personal CGT at 18–24% on the MVL distribution. FTCR reduces the UK liability by the Spanish tax already paid. |

## SECTION 3 — UK Tax Acronyms

| # | Acronym | Full Name | Description | Case Relevance |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 11 | **HMRC** | His Majesty's Revenue and Customs | The UK's national tax, payments, and customs authority. Administers UK income tax, corporation tax, capital gains tax, VAT, inheritance tax, and more. | Phil Harrison must report the MVL distribution on his UK Self Assessment (SA108). Los Romeros Limited must file a final CT600 corporate tax return declaring the Spanish property disposal. |
| 12 | **FTCR** | Foreign Tax Credit Relief | A mechanism under UK tax law allowing foreign tax already paid on a gain/income to be offset against the equivalent UK tax liability on the same gain/income, preventing double taxation. Governed by the UK-Spain Double Taxation Agreement (DTA). | If Spanish CGT of 19% was paid on the Lanzarote gain, FTCR can be claimed against the UK Corporation Tax (or personal CGT) on the same disposal. Where FTCR is "trapped" (cannot fully offset the UK liability), this is part of the quantum of loss in Phil's negligence claim against Wincham (~£16,800). |
| 13 | **DTA** | Double Taxation Agreement | A bilateral treaty between two countries that allocates taxing rights over cross-border income and gains to prevent the same income being taxed twice. The 2014 UK-Spain DTA is the operative treaty. | Under Article 13 of the UK-Spain DTA, Spain has primary taxing rights over gains on Spanish immovable property. The UK has secondary rights. FTCR bridges the two. |
| 14 | **IHT** | Inheritance Tax | UK tax levied on the estate of a deceased person above the available nil-rate bands, currently at 40%. | Once Phil receives the MVL distribution, it enters his UK estate and becomes subject to IHT. The Nil-Rate Band (NRB) and Residence Nil-Rate Band (RNRB) provide the primary exemptions. |
| 15 | **NRB** | Nil-Rate Band | The threshold below which no IHT is charged on a deceased's estate. Currently £325,000 (frozen until at least April 2028). Any unused NRB from a deceased spouse can be transferred to the survivor. | Phil Harrison's estate planning post-repatriation must account for how the MVL proceeds interact with the NRB and RNRB to quantity potential IHT exposure. |
| 16 | **RNRB** | Residence Nil-Rate Band | An additional IHT threshold (currently £175,000) available where a qualifying main residence is left to direct descendants (children/grandchildren). Combined with NRB and transferred bands, a couple can potentially pass £1 million free of IHT. | Applies where Phil's Sheffield home is left to Dean Harrison or other direct descendants. If the estate exceeds £2 million, the RNRB tapers at £2 for every £1 above that threshold. |
| 17 | **PET** | Potentially Exempt Transfer | An outright gift made during a person's lifetime which becomes fully exempt from IHT if the donor survives 7 years. If the donor dies within 7 years, taper relief reduces the IHT charge on a sliding scale. | A key IHT planning tool for Phil post-repatriation. Gifting part of the MVL proceeds to Dean or other family members starts the 7-year clock immediately. |
| 18 | **CLT** | Chargeable Lifetime Transfer | A gift into a discretionary trust (or to a company) that is immediately chargeable to IHT at 20% (the lifetime rate) to the extent it exceeds the NRB. Triggers a 7-year ITA clock for further relief. | Relevant if Phil were to consider placing funds into a trust. Unlike a PET, a CLT is immediately taxable (above NRB), and the full 40% rate applies to any additional tax if Phil dies within 7 years. |
| 19 | **BR** | Business Relief | An IHT relief providing 50–100% exemption on qualifying business assets (trading company shares, business interests). Does NOT apply to passive property-holding companies like Los Romeros Limited. | Not applicable to Phil's situation — Los Romeros is a passive property SPV, which HMRC explicitly excludes from Business Relief. |
| 20 | **AR** | Agricultural Relief | An IHT relief (50–100%) on qualifying agricultural property and land used for agricultural purposes. | Not applicable to this case — the Lanzarote property was residential, not agricultural. |
| 21 | **BADR** | Business Asset Disposal Relief | Formerly "Entrepreneurs' Relief" — reduces the CGT rate to 10% on up to £1 million lifetime qualifying gains from disposal of shares in or assets of a "trading company." | BADR does NOT apply to Los Romeros Limited because it held a passive residential property rather than conducting trading activities. The SIC code 70229 ("Management Consultancy") was a Wincham mis-classification. Phil's personal CGT rates on the MVL distribution will therefore be 18% or 24%. |
| 22 | **TAAR** | Transactions in Securities Anti-Avoidance Rule | A set of HMRC rules (s.684 ITA 2007) allowing HMRC to reclassify an MVL distribution as income rather than capital if they deem the liquidation was primarily tax-motivated and the shareholder carries on a similar trade within 2 years. | Low risk in Phil's case — the purpose of the MVL is genuine dissolution (the underlying asset has been sold), not a "phoenix" arrangement to recommence the same trade. |
| 23 | **CT** | Corporation Tax | UK tax on the profits of UK companies and some other organisations. Currently 19–25% depending on size of profits. | Los Romeros Limited must file a final CT600 return for the accounting period ending at dissolution, declaring the Spanish property disposal. FTCR will be applied to offset any dual-taxation. |
| 24 | **SA** | Self Assessment | HMRC's system for filing personal tax returns. A Self Assessment return (SA100) with the capital gains supplement (SA108) must be filed by Phil for the 2025/26 tax year to report his MVL distribution. Filing deadline: 31 January 2027. | Phil must report the capital gain arising from the MVL distribution on his Self Assessment return. The annual CGT allowance (£3,000 for 2025/26) will reduce the taxable amount. |
| 25 | **VAT** | Value Added Tax | UK consumption tax at 20% standard rate (and varying reduced rates). Wincham charged VAT on top of their annual service fees (£500 + £200 = £700 exc. VAT; £840 inc. 20% VAT per year). | Wincham/Adrem's annual fees were charged at £840 inc. VAT. Over approximately 6 years, total fees paid were ~£5,040 inc. VAT — part of the quantum of wasted fees in the profession negligence claim (£25,069 total historical costs). |
| 26 | **PAYE** | Pay As You Earn | HMRC's system for collecting income tax and National Insurance from employment income at source (via the employer). | Relevant to the MVL process — the IP must confirm no PAYE liabilities remain outstanding before distributing capital. Los Romeros had no employees, so PAYE is a nil item. |

## SECTION 4 — Corporate & Insolvency Law Acronyms

| # | Acronym | Full Name | Description | Case Relevance |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 27 | **MVL** | Members' Voluntary Liquidation | A formal, voluntary winding-up process for a *solvent* UK company.
