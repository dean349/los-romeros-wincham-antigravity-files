## PART 2 — THE RESCUE ROUTE: WHY IT STILL WORKS

### The Mechanism: Director's Loan Account → MVL
This is the legally recognised rescue pathway. It works as follows:

1. **Record the breach correctly** — The funds in Philip's personal account are formally recorded on Los Romeros Limited's books as an **overdrawn Director's Loan Account (DLA)**.
2. **Repay the DLA** — Philip transfers the funds back from his personal account into a designated **Los Romeros Limited corporate bank account** (currently being set up via Tide). This legally reverses the breach.
3. **MVL distributes correctly** — The Insolvency Practitioner (IP) appointed to conduct the Members' Voluntary Liquidation then distributes the funds from the corporate account as a **formal capital distribution on winding-up**.
4. **CGT treatment preserved** — The distribution is taxed as a capital gain (18%–24%) rather than income. All FTCR credits from Spanish CGT can be applied.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **This rescue route is well-established in UK insolvency practice. It is not a loophole or aggressive tax scheme.** An IP has a legal duty to call in overdrawn DLAs as company assets during an MVL. When Philip repays, the IP distributes. The final tax treatment is capital, not income.
