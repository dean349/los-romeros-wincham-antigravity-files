# CRS, AEAT Transparency & the Wincham Scheme — Research Note

> **Context:** This note provides detailed background on the sentence in the Lanzarote Repatriation dossier stating that Del Canto Chambers warned that CRS means AEAT will automatically receive information about UK-held accounts and companies from HMRC, making corporate wrapper structures increasingly transparent to Spanish authorities.

---

## 1. What Is CRS? (Common Reporting Standard)

The **Common Reporting Standard (CRS)** is an OECD-developed global framework for the **automatic exchange of financial account information** between tax authorities. It was designed to eliminate offshore tax evasion by making it impossible for individuals to hide assets in foreign jurisdictions.

- **Official OECD source:** [OECD CRS Portal](https://www.oecd.org/tax/automatic-exchange/common-reporting-standard/)
- **UK Legislation:** The International Tax Compliance Regulations 2015 (SI 2015/878), which enacted CRS into UK law
- **HMRC guidance:** [HMRC CRS Implementation](https://www.gov.uk/guidance/common-reporting-standard)

### How It Works (UK → Spain Corridor)

1. UK financial institutions (banks, investment platforms, accountants holding client funds) **identify Spanish tax residents** among their account holders
2. They report those accounts to **HMRC**
3. HMRC **automatically transmits** this data to **AEAT** (Agencia Estatal de Administración Tributaria), Spain's tax authority
4. The reverse also applies — Spanish banks report UK residents to AEAT → HMRC

**First exchanges occurred in September 2017**, covering the 2016 calendar year. Both UK and Spain were "early adopter" jurisdictions.

## 2. CRS and the "Look-Through" Provision on Corporate Wrappers

This is the mechanism that makes the **Wincham scheme** specifically vulnerable.

### Passive Non-Financial Entities (Passive NFEs)

Under CRS, all entities (companies) are classified as either:
- **Active NFE** — a company engaged in real business activity (manufacturing, services, etc.)
- **Passive NFE** — a company that primarily receives passive income (rent, dividends, interest) or holds passive assets

A **UK limited company holding a Spanish holiday villa** — with no employees, no trading activity, generating only rental income or sitting idle — is almost certainly classified as a **Passive NFE**.

### The Critical "Look-Through" Rule

When a bank holds an account for a **Passive NFE**, CRS mandates that the bank **look through the company** to identify the **Controlling Persons** (beneficial owners — i.e., the shareholders/directors).

If those Controlling Persons are **Spanish tax residents**, the bank must:
- Report their identity (name, address, date of birth, NIE/TIN) to HMRC
- HMRC then passes this to **AEAT automatically**

> **In plain English:** The Spanish tax authority is told: *"This UK company has a bank account; the beneficial owner is a Spanish-resident person who holds this property."*

The corporate veil is pierced for tax information purposes.

- [OECD CRS Commentary on Controlling Persons (Pictet Bank CRS Guide)](https://www.pictet.com/int-en/crs.html)
- [HMRC Guidance: CRS Meaning of Controlling Person](https://www.gov.uk/guidance/common-reporting-standard)

## 4. The Wincham Scheme — What It Was

The **Wincham scheme** (marketed by Wincham Consultants / Wincham International Limited) involved:

1. A Spanish property owner being advised to transfer ownership of their Spanish villa into a **UK private limited company**
2. The owner/family becoming shareholders and directors of that company
3. The argument was that upon death, beneficiaries would inherit **shares in the UK company** rather than Spanish real estate directly
4. Wincham claimed this would **avoid or minimise Spanish Inheritance Tax (IHT)** — the "Spanish Property Inheritance Tax Ticking Time Bomb" as they marketed it

### Why CRS Destroys This Architecture

| Wincham's Claimed Protection | CRS Reality |
|---|---|
| AEAT would only see "a UK company owns a Spanish property" | AEAT now automatically receives details of the UK company's controlling persons via HMRC |
| Inheritance passes as UK company shares, not Spanish real estate | AEAT cross-references CRS data against Modelo 720 (overseas asset declarations) |
| UK corporate structure is opaque to Spanish authorities | Passive NFE look-through rules expose beneficial ownership |
| Tax residency of beneficial owner not visible to Spain | Controlling Person reporting transmits NIE, address, and identity of Spanish-resident owners |

## 7. Additional Transparency Levers Beyond CRS

CRS is not the only mechanism AEAT now uses. The following compound the transparency risk:

| Mechanism | Effect on Wincham Structures |
|---|---|
| **EU 4th/5th AML Directives** | EU-wide registers of Ultimate Beneficial Owners (UBOs) of companies |
| **UK Register of Overseas Entities (ROE)** | Since Aug 2022, UK Companies House requires overseas entities owning UK (and historically foreign) property to register beneficial owners |
| **Modelo 720** | Spanish residents must declare overseas assets >€50,000 — includes shares in foreign companies holding Spanish property |
| **Modelo 210** | Non-resident companies owning Spanish property must file annual IRNR returns — failure flags AEAT investigation |
| **Spanish Non-Residents' Capital Gains Retention** | 3% of sale price withheld at source on disposal by non-resident companies |
| **AEAT Tax Inspection Plans** | Annual plans specifically target corporate-owned real estate without genuine business substance |

## 8. Summary: Why the Paragraph Is Accurate

The paragraph in question states:

> *"CRS means AEAT will automatically receive information about UK-held accounts and companies from HMRC, making corporate wrapper structures increasingly transparent to Spanish authorities."*

This is factually correct and supported by:

1. ✅ **OECD CRS standard** — automatic exchange between UK (HMRC) and Spain (AEAT) since 2017
2. ✅ **Passive NFE look-through rule** — UK property-holding companies with no real business activity are classified as Passive NFEs; their beneficial owners are automatically reported
3. ✅ **Del Canto Chambers** — published direct warnings that AEAT uses CRS data to scrutinise UK structures, and that structures without genuine substance are targeted
4. ✅ **AEAT Binding Consultation V3350-13** — Spanish tax authority formally rejected the Wincham-type structure as tax evasion
5. ✅ **Lawbird, Olive Press, Blevins Franks** — independent corroboration that the scheme is legally unsound and increasingly visible to AEAT
