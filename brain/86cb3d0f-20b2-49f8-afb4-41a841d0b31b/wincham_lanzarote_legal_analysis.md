# Wincham Group — Lanzarote / Canary Islands Legal Authority Analysis

## Forensic Examination: Was Wincham Legally Permitted to Operate in Lanzarote Without a Registered Office?

**Prepared:** April 2026  
**Purpose:** Legal dossier support — Professional Negligence & Breach of Contract proceedings  
**Jurisdiction:** Lanzarote (Las Palmas province), Canary Islands, Kingdom of Spain

## Executive Summary

Research confirms that Wincham Group operated fiscal representation, property advisory, Spanish tax filing, and estate planning services for UK non-residents in Lanzarote **without any registered Spanish office, any Spanish-licensed professional on staff, and without registration with the relevant Spanish tax authorities**. This was unlawful under a minimum of **six interlocking legal frameworks** spanning Spanish national law, Canarian autonomous community law, and post-Brexit third-country reclassification rules. The exposure crystallised in full on **1 January 2021** when the UK ceased to be treated as an EU Member State — but earlier requirements also applied from their inception.

## Part 1: Lanzarote's Unique Legal Position — The Canary Islands Are Not Like the Rest of Spain

This is critical context that most UK operators miss, and it is the **first legal trap** Wincham fell into.

### 1.1 The Régimen Económico y Fiscal (REF) — Law 19/1994

The Canary Islands operate under a constitutionally protected special economic and fiscal regime established by **Ley 19/1994, de 6 de julio, de modificación del Régimen Económico y Fiscal de Canarias** (BOE-A-1994-16549 is the principal REF statute). This is not merely a tax incentive — it is a **separate legal ecosystem** that applies concurrently with but sometimes supersedes mainland Spanish law.

**Key features directly relevant to Wincham:**

| Feature | Mainland Spain | Canary Islands (Lanzarote) |
|---|---|---|
| Indirect tax | IVA (21% standard) | **IGIC** — Impuesto General Indirecto Canario (7% standard) |
| Tax administration body | AEAT (national) | **ATC** — Administración Tributaria Canaria (regional) |
| Property indirect taxes | ITP / AJD | **ITP Canario** (different rates) |
| Corporate tax incentives | Standard IS rates | ZEC zone, RIC deduction, DIC |
| VAT/IGIC territory | EU harmonised VAT zone | **Outside EU VAT harmonisation** |

**Forensic implication:** A UK company advising Lanzarote property clients was required to understand and comply with **two overlapping tax regimes** (AEAT national + ATC regional), not one. Wincham's marketing materials referenced "Spanish tax" generically, with no disclosed Canarian specialisation.

### 1.2 Lanzarote's Island-Level Government: The Cabildo

Lanzarote (like all Canary Islands) has an **island government — the Cabildo de Lanzarote** — with autonomous competencies over local taxation, including:
- **IBI (Impuesto sobre Bienes Inmuebles)** — collected via municipal Ayuntamientos (Arrecife, Tías, Yaiza, San Bartolomé, Haría, Tinajo, Teguise)  
- **Plusvalía Municipal** — levied per municipality  
- **Tourist regulation** — Vivienda Vacacional (VV) licensing  
- **Local licensing** — economic activity declarations (IAE) where applicable

Any professional advisor filing or advising on these obligations operates under a **three-layer legal architecture**: national (AEAT/BOE), autonomous community (Parlamento de Canarias/ATC), and municipal (Cabildo/Ayuntamiento). Wincham had no presence at any of these three levels.

## Part 2: Spanish National Law — The Framework Wincham Violated

### 2.1 Ley 58/2003 General Tributaria — Article 47: Mandatory Representative Appointment

**The statute:** *Artículo 47, Ley 58/2003, de 17 de diciembre, General Tributaria (LGT)*

This provision requires non-resident taxpayers to appoint **a representative with domicile in Spain** when:
1. They operate via a permanent establishment in Spain;
2. Spanish tax law expressly mandates it (as it does under IRNR law for third-country non-residents); or
3. The tax administration requires it due to the nature or amount of income.

**Sanciones (penalties) for non-compliance:**
- **€2,000 fine** for failure to appoint a representative
- **€6,000 fine** where the non-resident is in a country without effective tax information exchange

The UK, post-Brexit, **does not have automatic bilateral tax information exchange arrangements** equivalent to those within the EU, placing it in the higher-exposure bracket.

**Wincham's position:** Wincham held itself out as *being* the fiscal representative for its UK clients but was itself a UK company without Spanish residency, a Spanish NIF (empresa no residente), or a registered AEAT representative. It was the **wrongdoer posing as the remedy.**

### 2.2 Real Decreto Legislativo 5/2004 (IRNR) — Article 10: Fiscal Representative for Non-Residents

**The statute:** *Artículo 10, Real Decreto Legislativo 5/2004, de 5 de marzo, por el que se aprueba el texto refundido de la Ley del Impuesto sobre la Renta de No Residentes*

Under IRNR Art. 10, non-resident entities from **third countries outside the EU/EEA** (which now includes the UK from 1 January 2021) **must** appoint a fiscal representative in Spain when:

1. Operating via a permanent establishment;
2. Providing services, technical assistance, or economic activities without a PE;
3. Holding property in Spanish territory (including Canary Islands property);
4. The AEAT requires it due to nature or amount of income.

**Pre-Brexit (before 1 Jan 2021):** EU freedom of services allowed UK companies to act as fiscal advisors in Spain without permanent establishment **provided** they complied with host-country professional regulation. Wincham never demonstrated this compliance.

**Post-Brexit (from 1 Jan 2021):** The UK became a **"tercer país" (third country)**. All EU-based exemptions disappeared. Wincham was **unlicensed** to act as fiscal representative for UK non-residents in Lanzarote from this date.

### 2.3 Ley 17/2009 "Ley Paraguas" (Services Directive Transposition) — And Its Explicit Exclusion

**The statute:** *Ley 17/2009, de 23 de noviembre, sobre el libre acceso a las actividades de servicios y su ejercicio*

Spain's transposition of EU Directive 2006/123/EC (the Services Directive) permits EU-established professionals to provide services temporarily in Spain without a permanent establishment. HOWEVER:

> **Ley 17/2009 explicitly excludes the fiscal/tax domain from its scope of free access.** Tax advisory and fiscal representation are carved out from the free-service provision principle precisely because of the professional responsibility entailed.

**Forensic implication:** Wincham cannot rely on the Services Directive / Ley 17/2009 as a defence for its unregulated cross-border operations. The statutory exclusion of fiscal services is unambiguous.

### 2.4 Artículo 545 Código Civil / Colegio de Gestores Administrativos — Professional Monopoly

Spain maintains a **regulated professional monopoly** for representation before public administrations. *Gestores Administrativos* (regulated by the Colegio de Gestores Administrativos de Las Palmas, C/ León y Castillo 20, 2º, 35003 Las Palmas de Gran Canaria) are the specifically licensed professionals authorised to:
- Represent clients before AEAT
- Submit Modelo 210 / 211 / 036 filings
- Act as *colaborador social* (social collaborator) with AEAT

An unregistered UK company **cannot legally be a "collaborador social" with AEAT** — this status requires formal registration and accreditation with the Colegio. Wincham was not registered.

## Part 3: Canarian Autonomous Law — The Second Layer of Violation

### 3.1 The IGIC Framework — Ley 20/1991 and Ley 4/2012

**The statutes:**
- *Ley 20/1991, de 7 de junio, de modificación de los aspectos fiscales del Régimen Económico Fiscal de Canarias* (IGIC framework)
- *Ley 4/2012, de 25 de junio, de medidas administrativas y fiscales* (amendments to IGIC)

IGIC (Impuesto General Indirecto Canario) is **not the same as IVA**. It is administered by the **Administración Tributaria Canaria (ATC)** — a separate body from AEAT — at:

> Viceconsejería de Hacienda, Gobierno de Canarias  
> Website: https://atc.gobiernodecanarias.org/atc/

Any non-resident company charging for professional services delivered to Canarian clients (or managed on behalf of Canarian-based assets) may trigger IGIC registration obligations. The mandatory forms are:

| Form | Purpose | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| **Modelo 400** | IGIC registration (Alta Censal) | On commencement of activity |
| **Modelo 420** | IGIC quarterly self-assessment | Quarterly |
| **Modelo 425** | IGIC annual summary | Annual |

**Wincham's position:** There is **no record** of Wincham or Agere International Ltd (FKA Wincham International) filing Modelo 400 registration with the ATC for Canarian IGIC purposes. Operating without ATC registration while providing professional services to Canarian-sited property owners constitutes an unregistered economic activity in the Canary Islands.

### 3.2 The Post-Brexit IGIC Services Localisation Rule

The Canary Islands are **outside the EU VAT harmonisation zone** (Protocol 2 to the Act of Accession of Spain). This means:
- Trade between mainland UK and the Canary Islands is treated as **import/export**
- The "reverse charge" VAT mechanism that EU B2B services use does not apply in the same way
- UK companies providing professional services to Canarian clients are subject to **full IGIC assessment by the ATC**

Post-Brexit, since 1 January 2021, the ATC treats UK-sourced professional services to Canarian businesses/individuals under third-country rules. Wincham should have been collecting and remitting IGIC to the ATC — it was not, and had no ATC registration to do so.

### 3.3 Parlamento de Canarias — Decree 113/2015: Tourism Regulation

*Decreto 113/2015, de 22 de mayo, por el que se aprueba el Reglamento de las viviendas vacacionales de la Comunidad Autónoma de Canarias* governs Vivienda Vacacional (VV) holiday rental licensing. If Wincham acted as rental manager for any client properties in Lanzarote, they would have been required to interface with:
1. The **Cabildo de Lanzarote's tourism department** for VV registration
2. The **Ayuntamiento** for local licences
3. The **ATC** for IGIC on rental income

An unlicensed, unregistered UK company acting as rental manager for Canarian-sited properties is in breach of this autonomous community regulation.

## Part 6: Specific Legal Violations Identified

### Violation 1: Operating as Unlicensed Fiscal Representative (Post-Brexit)
**Legal basis:** IRNR Art. 10 (RDL 5/2004); LGT Art. 47 (Ley 58/2003)  
**Period:** 1 January 2021 — present  
**Effect:** All Modelo 210/211 filings submitted by Wincham on behalf of UK clients post-Brexit may be voidable; clients were never represented by a legally qualified Spanish entity  
**Sanction exposure (clients):** €2,000 per client per year for absence of compliant fiscal representative

### Violation 2: Unlicensed Professional Activity — AEAT Colaborador Social Impersonation
**Legal basis:** AEAT Colaborador Social Regulations; Colegio de Gestores Administrativos  
**Period:** Throughout operations  
**Effect:** Wincham lacked the formal AEAT accreditation to submit filings on behalf of clients; any Spanish-domiciled gestor they engaged without disclosure to clients constitutes a sub-contracting without informed consent

### Violation 3: IGIC Non-Registration with ATC (Administración Tributaria Canaria)
**Legal basis:** Ley 20/1991 (IGIC); Modelo 400 registration requirement  
**Period:** Throughout operations in Canary Islands  
**Effect:** Unregistered professional service provision in the Canary Islands without ATC registration; potential IGIC liability on fees charged to Canarian-sited clients

### Violation 4: Services Directive Does Not Protect Tax Services
**Legal basis:** Ley 17/2009 Art. 2 (exclusion of fiscal services); Directive 2006/123/EC  
**Period:** Throughout operations  
**Effect:** Wincham cannot rely on "EU free provision of services" as a defence for its unlicensed tax advisory operations — the statute expressly excludes this sector

### Violation 5: Post-Brexit Third-Country Reclassification — No Adapted Business Model
**Legal basis:** IRNR Art. 10 (as amended by Ley 11/2021); LGT Art. 47  
**Period:** 1 January 2021 — present  
**Effect:** Wincham continued operating as if in the EU after Brexit without restructuring — no Spanish branch, no local qualified representative, no ATC registration. This is not a technical breach; it is a **systemic, prolonged failure to adapt** while continuing to charge clients.

### Violation 6: Canarian Cierre de Hoja Registral — Wincham International Spain SL
**Legal basis:** Código de Comercio; Ley 17/1999 (Registro Mercantil)  
**Previously confirmed:** Wincham International Spain SL has *cierre de hoja registral* (closed registration), meaning it cannot perform any legal acts under Spanish company law until the closure is lifted  
**Combined with absence of Lanzarote office:** Wincham's **only** Spanish legal vehicle is legally inoperative. There is no other Spanish entity capable of executing its Canarian operations lawfully.

In the Letter of Claim / Particulars of Claim, the Canarian violations should be pleaded as follows:

> *"The Defendant held itself out as competent and authorised to provide fiscal representation and tax advisory services for UK non-residents with property situate in Lanzarote in the Canary Islands. In fact, the Defendant: (i) held no registration with the Administración Tributaria Canaria; (ii) held no IGIC registration (Modelo 400); (iii) was not registered with the Colegio de Gestores Administrativos de Las Palmas; (iv) had no registered commercial presence in the Canary Islands; and (v) from 1 January 2021, was not qualified to act as fiscal representative for UK non-residents under Spanish law as a third-country entity. The Claimant reasonably relied upon the Defendant's representations of competence and suffered loss thereby."*
