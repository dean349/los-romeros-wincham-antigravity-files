### OPTION 2 — COST PER LEAD / CPL
*Run a landing page (WinchamActionGroup.com) + direct mail campaign. Pass qualified leads (victims who have expressed interest) to the law firm at a fixed price per lead.*

| Scenario | Conversion Rate | Leads Generated | Fee per Lead | **Dean's Gross Income** |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Conservative | 5% | 78 leads | £200 | **£15,600** |
| Base Case | 10% | 157 leads | £300 | **£47,100** |

**Pros:** Low risk — you get paid for qualified interest, not signed clients.
**Cons:** Lower per-unit fee. Law firm bears conversion risk and may dispute lead quality.
**Verdict:** ⚠️ Acceptable as a first step. Negotiate a hybrid (CPL + small CPA kicker).

### OPTION 3 — COST PER ACQUISITION / CPA ⭐ RECOMMENDED NEAR-TERM
*Dean runs all marketing. Law firm handles consultations. Dean is paid only when a claimant signs a formal DBA (retainer to sue Wincham).*

| Scenario | Conversion Rate | Signed Claimants | Fee per Sign | **Dean's Gross Income** |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Conservative (5%) | 5% | 78 signed | £750 | **£58,500** |
| Base Case (10%) | 10% | 157 signed | £1,000 | **£157,000** |
| Optimistic (15%) | 15% | 235 signed | £1,250 | **£293,750** |
| Maximum (20%) | 20% | 313 signed | £1,500 | **£469,500** |

**At 10% conversion with £1,000 CPA:**
- 157 signed claimants × £1,000 = **£157,000 personal income** to Dean
- Aggregate claim pool: 157 × £120,000 = **£18.8 million litigation**
- Law firm DBA fee (25%): **£4.7 million** — from which Dean's CPA is a bargain

**Pros:** Maximum leverage. Law firm accepts the settlement risk; Dean gets paid per confirmed client. Highly scalable.
**Cons:** Income delayed to DBA-signing stage (4–8 weeks post-outreach campaign).
**Verdict:** ✅ Best standalone model if not yet FCA-authorised.

### OPTION 4 — MARKETING RETAINER + CPA ⭐ RECOMMENDED COMBINATION
*Dean charges the law firm a monthly retainer to cover campaign management and direct mail costs, plus a CPA kicker per signed client. This is Dean positioning himself as the exclusive marketing agency.*

#### Year 1 Financial Model

| Component | Conservative | Base Case | Optimistic |
|---|---|---|---|
| Monthly retainer × 12 months | £3,000 × 12 = **£36,000** | £4,000 × 12 = **£48,000** | £5,000 × 12 = **£60,000** |

| **Year 1 Total** | **£59,400** | **£95,100** | **£130,500** |

> [!TIP]
> **Negotiate the retainer to cover hard costs** (direct mail printing ~£2–4k, ad spend ~£1–2k/month). This means the retainer is effectively profit-neutral on costs, and the CPA income is pure margin.

**Pros:** Immediate cashflow from day one. Risk-shared with law firm. Positions Dean as a long-term commercial partner.
**Cons:** Retainer requires an active service relationship (monthly reporting, campaign management).
**Verdict:** ✅ Best opening pitch to a law firm — gives them comfort while Dean builds track record.

### OPTION 5 — REVENUE SHARE / EQUITY IN DBA FEE INCOME 🏆 MAXIMUM UPSIDE
*Rather than a flat CPA, Dean negotiates a percentage of the law firm's Damages-Based Agreement (DBA) fee income — i.e., a cut of their 25–30% of all settlements.*

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **This requires FCA authorisation as a Claims Management Company (CMC)** to be conducted compliantly as the primary commercial model. Without FCA authorisation, this must be structured via a solicitor-introduced revenue-sharing arrangement — which some firms will accept commercially.

| Maximum (20% conv.) | 313 | £170,000 | £53.2M | £13.3M | **£1,330,000** | **£1,995,000** |

**The maximum scenario with full CMC authorisation:**
- Dean operates as a licensed CMC, charging his own DBA of 20–30%
- 313 claimants × £170,000 average = £53.2M claim pool
- At 25% DBA: **£13.3M in fees — all to Dean's CMC**
