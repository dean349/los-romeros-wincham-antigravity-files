## PART 3 — WHY AN MVL IS THE ONLY VIABLE STRATEGY

### 3.1 Comparing Liquidation Routes

**Option A: DS01 Voluntary Strike-Off**
A DS01 is designed for dormant companies with no assets. For a company holding significant cash:
- HMRC will object to the strike-off application (they monitor all dissolutions)
- Any undistributed cash at the moment of dissolution becomes **Bona Vacantia** (literally "ownerless goods") — the assets vest in the Crown (Treasury Solicitor)
- Recovering Bona Vacantia assets from the Crown is expensive, time-consuming, and not guaranteed
- **Verdict: Wholly inappropriate and dangerously wrong for Los Romeros**

**Option B: Creditors' Voluntary Liquidation (CVL)**
A CVL is for insolvent companies. Los Romeros is solvent. Using a CVL would be legally incorrect and potentially fraudulent.

**Option C: Members' Voluntary Liquidation (MVL)**
- The legally correct mechanism for a solvent company with assets to distribute
- Produces a formal *capital distribution* to shareholders, taxed as a capital gain (not income)
- Administered by a licensed Insolvency Practitioner (IP) under the Insolvency Act 1986
- HMRC recognises and accepts MVL distributions as capital receipts
- **Verdict: The only correct route**

### 3.2 Dividends vs. MVL — The Full Tax Comparison

If Philip simply extracted the cash as dividends instead of using an MVL:

| Band | Dividend Tax Rate (2025/26) | Which rate applies to Philip? |
|---|---|---|
| Basic Rate | 8.75% | Only on first ~£50k above income threshold |
| Higher Rate | 33.75% | On remainder to ~£125k |
| Additional Rate | **39.35%** | On everything above £125,140 |

Given the scale of funds in Los Romeros, Philip would be firmly in the Additional Rate band for the bulk of any dividend — making a dividend extraction **catastrophically tax-inefficient**.

An MVL capital distribution is taxed at **18%** (basic rate) or **24%** (higher rate) — a very significant saving.

### 5.1 Business Asset Disposal Relief (BADR) — Does It Apply?

BADR (previously Entrepreneurs' Relief) can reduce the CGT rate to 14% (or 18% from April 2026) on up to a **£1 million lifetime limit** of qualifying gains. The eligibility conditions are:

1. Philip must be an **officer or employee** of Los Romeros ✅ (he is sole director)
2. Philip must hold at least **5% of ordinary shares** ✅ (he holds 25–50%)
3. The company must be a **trading company** (or holding company of a trading group)

**The third condition is the critical one — and it is almost certainly FAILED.**

HMRC defines a "trading company" as one whose activities do not include, to a **substantial extent** (>20% test), non-trading activities. Los Romeros Limited:
- Was incorporated **solely to hold one Spanish residential villa**
- Had SIC code **70229** (management consultancy) — a generic code applied by Wincham, not reflecting any genuine trading activity
- Generated zero trading income throughout its life
- Its **only** asset was the Spanish property — a passive investment

**BADR VERDICT: Almost certainly NOT eligible.** HMRC would classify Los Romeros as an investment/property-holding company, which fails the trading company test entirely. Standard CGT rates of 18% or 24% will apply. **Philip's UK Chartered Tax Adviser must confirm this before the IP makes any distribution.**

### 5.2 Targeted Anti-Avoidance Rule (TAAR) — The Phoenix Risk

The TAAR (introduced 2016, s.396B ITTOIA 2005) was designed to stop "phoenixing" — liquidating a company to extract profits as capital, then reconstituting a similar business. HMRC can reclassify capital distributions as income if **all four conditions** are met:

| Condition | Test | Philip's Position |
|---|---|---|
| **A** | 5%+ interest in company | ✅ Yes (holds 25-50%) |
| **B** | Company was "close" (controlled by ≤5 persons or directors) | ✅ Yes — Los Romeros was close throughout |
| **C** | Continues to carry on the **same or similar trade** within 2 years of distribution | ❌ **DOES NOT APPLY** |
| **D** | Main purpose was avoidance of Income Tax | ❌ **DOES NOT APPLY** |

**TAAR VERDICT: TAAR does NOT apply to Philip Harrison's situation.** Los Romeros had no genuine trading activity, and Philip is a retired individual who will not reconstitute any similar business. Conditions C and D both fail. The MVL is a legitimate, tax-appropriate winding-up of a defunct property-holding vehicle — precisely what MVL was designed for.

> [!NOTE]
> The TAAR is aimed squarely at contractor IT professionals or service business owners who liquidate a company and immediately continue working through a new entity. This situation is diametrically different — it is the straightforward wind-up of a passive property holding company following the sale of its only asset.

### 6.1 Spain: The AEAT Retention and Modelo 210

Los Romeros is a UK non-resident company for Spanish tax purposes. The Spanish disposal of a non-resident's property triggers:

**Step 1 — Modelo 211 (3% retention at source):**
- The buyer's notary withheld **3% × €315,000 = €9,450** and paid it directly to AEAT
- This is a *mandatory advance payment* against the final CGT liability
- **Confirm this was filed and the receipt obtained from the Spanish gestoria/notary**

**Step 2 — Modelo 210 (Final CGT return):**

> [!CAUTION]
> **CRITICAL DEADLINE: Modelo 210 must be filed within 4 months of the sale date (20 March 2026) — i.e., by approximately 20 July 2026.** Missing this deadline forfeits the right to reclaim any overpaid AEAT retention. This is a hard deadline under Spanish tax law.

The Spanish CGT calculation formula:

```
€315,000  (Gross disposal price — verified)
  Less:  Acquisition cost (2019 escritura value)
  Less:  Purchase costs (ITP/VAT, notary, registry 2019)
  Less:  Capital improvements (documented invoices)
  Less:  Sale costs (gestoria, agency commission)
= Net Taxable Gain
× 19% = Gross Spanish CGT
  Less:  €9,450 (3% retention already paid via Modelo 211)
= Balance payable (or refund claim if retention > CGT)
```

### 6.2 UK: Corporation Tax on the Disposal

As a UK-registered company, Los Romeros must declare the Spanish property disposal on a UK Corporation Tax return (CT600). The gain is calculated in **GBP**, using HMRC spot rates on:
- **Acquisition date (2019):** The EUR/GBP rate on the date the property was legally acquired
- **Disposal date (20 March 2026):** The EUR/GBP rate on the day the escritura was signed

### 6.3 Foreign Tax Credit Relief (FTCR) — Avoiding Double Taxation

The UK-Spain Double Taxation Agreement (DTA) gives Spain primary taxing rights on the property. However, as a UK-resident company, Los Romeros is also liable to UK Corporation Tax on its worldwide gains.

**FTCR prevents double taxation.** The mechanism:
1. Calculate the UK Corporation Tax liability on the Spanish disposal gain
2. Calculate the Spanish IRNR tax paid (via Modelo 210)
3. Claim the **lower of** the foreign tax paid OR the UK CT liability on that specific gain as a credit against the UK CT bill

**Alternative election:** Instead of a credit, Los Romeros can elect to **deduct** the Spanish tax from the taxable gain in the UK. This is sometimes preferable depending on whether the company has other losses or the relative tax rates. A specialist cross-border CTA must advise.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **This calculation is complex and must be done by a UK-Spain dual-qualified Chartered Tax Adviser (CTA) BEFORE the MVL distribution.** If FTCR is not correctly claimed, Los Romeros (and by extension Philip) could pay UK Corporation Tax AND Spanish CGT on the same gain — effectively being taxed twice.

### 6.4 Phantom FX Currency Gains

HMRC requires all gains to be calculated in **GBP** at the actual historical exchange rates. If the EUR weakened against GBP between the 2019 acquisition and the March 2026 disposal, the GBP equivalent of the proceeds will be **larger** than expected — generating a "phantom gain" in Sterling even if no additional value was created in Euro terms. This phantom gain is fully taxable under UK law.

**Action:** The appointed CTA must use the Bank of England historical EUR/GBP spot rate for both the acquisition date and 20 March 2026 to calculate the precise GBP gain.
