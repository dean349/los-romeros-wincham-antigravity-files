## 2. Why an MVL is the Only Viable Strategy

An MVL is a formal, solvent liquidation process administered by a licensed Insolvency Practitioner (IP). 

### Dividends vs. MVL Capital Distribution
If Philip were to simply pay himself the cash as a dividend:
*   Dividends are taxed as income. 
*   For a retired taxpayer, extracting hundreds of thousands of pounds will instantly push him into the Additional Rate band.
*   **Income Tax on Dividends:** Basic rate 8.75%, Higher rate 33.75%, Additional rate 39.35%.

By using an MVL:
*   The HMRC clears the distribution as a "capital" event.
*   **Capital Gains Tax (CGT) Rates:** 18% (basic rate) and 24% (higher rate). 

> [!CAUTION]
> **Business Asset Disposal Relief (BADR) Does Not Apply**
> While BADR (formerly Entrepreneurs' Relief) can reduce CGT to 10%, Los Romeros Limited was a passive property-holding Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV), classified under SIC code 70229. It fails the HMRC "trading company" test. The standard CGT rates of 18% and 24% will apply to this distribution.

## 3. Critical Cross-Border Tax Overlaps (The "FTCR" & "Phantom Gains")

Because the property was in Spain but the company is in the UK, the MVL process intersects heavily with cross-border tax laws before the IP can legally distribute the funds:

### A. Foreign Tax Credit Relief (FTCR)
Spain has the primary taxing rights on the property sale (19% IRNR). The Spanish Notary withheld 3% of the gross price via Modelo 211, and a Modelo 210 must be filed by ~20 July 2026 to settle the final Spanish Capital Gains Tax.
**Crucially:** Los Romeros Limited must also declare this disposal for UK Corporation Tax (CT). Under the UK-Spain Double Taxation Agreement, the Spanish tax paid must be utilized as a credit (FTCR) to offset the UK Corporation Tax liability *before* the liquidator calculates the final divisible cash pool.

### B. Phantom Currency Gains
HMRC requires all corporate gains to be calculated in **GBP (£)** using the historical spot rates on the exact date of acquisition (2019) and the date of disposal (20 March 2026). If the Euro weakened or strengthened against the Pound during this 7-year holding period, Los Romeros may have generated a taxable "phantom gain" in the UK, even if the property's value in Euros barely moved.

3. **Settle Final Debts:** Settle any remaining Wincham exit fees or accounting invoices. 

### Stage 3: Capital Distribution (Weeks 12–20)

2. **Personal Self-Assessment:** Philip's accountant reports the MVL distribution as a capital disposal on the `SA108 Capital Gains supplement` of his personal tax return.
3. **Pay Personal CGT:** Philip pays the resultant 18%/24% CGT to HMRC by 31 January 2027.

### Stage 4: Formal Dissolution (Weeks 18–26)

2. **Strike Off:** Los Romeros Limited is officially dissolved and ceases to exist.
