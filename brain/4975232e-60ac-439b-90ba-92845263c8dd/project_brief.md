## Part 1 — What Is This All About? (For Philip Harrison)

Dad — this section is written specifically so you understand what your son Dean has been building on your behalf over the past several weeks. It's complex, but broken down simply:

### The Starting Point

You own a villa in **Lanzarote, Spain** — held inside a UK limited company called **Los Romeros Limited** (Company No. 06993349). The company was set up for you in **2009 by Wincham Accountants**, a Congleton-based firm that specialises in helping UK people buy Spanish property through a corporate structure. The idea they sold you was that owning the villa through a UK company would **save you 16.5% on Spanish property transaction taxes** and make passing the property on to your children cheaper (inheritance tax savings).

The villa has now been **sold for €315,000 net proceeds**. The money has arrived — but there are several big problems that need fixing, and a significant sum of money potentially owed back to you from Wincham.

---

### The Five Things Dean Has Been Working On

#### 1. 🇪🇸 Working Out Your Spanish Tax Bill (Capital Gains Tax)

When you sold the Lanzarote villa, **Spain took a 3% retention** (~€9,450) from the sale price immediately via the notary. This was sent to the Spanish tax authority (AEAT). Now, after the sale, you need to file a Spanish tax return (called **Modelo 210**) to declare the actual capital gain.

**Dean has calculated:**
- What the property originally cost vs. what it sold for
- What the true Spanish Capital Gains Tax rate is for non-residents (19%)
- How much of that retention you may be able to claim back from AEAT
- How Spanish CGT interacts with UK CGT (via Foreign Tax Credit Relief — FTCR), so you don't pay tax twice

This work produced a full CGT report published online.

---

#### 2. 🏦 The Banking Mess Wincham Created

When the Spanish sale completed, the €315,000 should have gone into a **Los Romeros Limited business bank account** — because it's the *company's* money, not your personal money.

Wincham, however, directed the money into **your personal HSBC bank account** instead.

This is a serious problem because:
- It means the company (Los Romeros Ltd) has officially "lent" you the money as a **Director's Loan**
- If that loan isn't repaid to the company within 9 months, HMRC can charge the company **32.5% Corporation Tax** on the whole amount (called Section 455 tax)
- It also creates complications for the wind-down (MVL) of the company

Dean has **documented this error**, created letters and action plans to correct it, and researched how to set up a proper business bank account (Tide or Starling) so the money is correctly recorded — even at this late stage.

---

#### 3. 💼 Winding Up the Company Properly (MVL — Members' Voluntary Liquidation)

Because you've now sold the villa, Los Romeros Limited has no real purpose anymore. The cleanest, most tax-efficient way to extract the £265,000+ sitting inside it is through a process called a **Members' Voluntary Liquidation (MVL)**.

An MVL allows you to:
- **Formally close the company** through a licensed insolvency practitioner (IP)
- Extract the money as a **capital distribution** rather than income
- Pay Capital Gains Tax at **10–20% rather than up to 45% income tax**
- Take advantage of your annual CGT allowance

Dean has built scenario planning models comparing every possible option — reinvesting in another Spanish property through the company, taking dividends, or doing an MVL — and the MVL wins every time.

---

#### 4. ⚖️ Suing Wincham Accountants for Professional Negligence

This is the biggest and most complex workstream. In plain terms: **Wincham may have mis-sold you the entire corporate structure**, and there are now serious questions about whether their advice was honest, competent, or legal.

The key failures Dean has documented:

- Wincham claimed you'd save 16.5% Spanish property tax (ITP) by owning via a UK company. Evidence from the Spanish tax authority suggests **AEAT does not accept this**
- Their promises about inheritance tax savings are now legally questionable after Brexit changed UK/Spain rules
- They sent **€315,000 of company money to your personal bank account** — which is either incompetent or irregular
- Wincham operates through a complex web of companies where **the "Wincham" brand customers trust is not actually the regulated entity**
- Companies House records show Wincham has run this scheme for **782+ UK limited companies** (meaning ~1,564 victims in the same position as you)

Dean has estimated the damages owed to you alone at between **£86,310 and £149,807**.

---

#### 5. 💰 The Group Litigation Opportunity (The Business Side)

While building all of this evidence for your case, Dean realised something: the Wincham scheme affected **over 1,500 people** across the UK — all in roughly the same position as you. This is prime territory for a **Group Litigation Order (GLO)** — where all victims bring a single legal action together.

Dean has been building a commercial structure to:
- Approach major UK law firms (e.g., Edwin Coe LLP, Leigh Day) with the victim database
- License the data to a law firm or claims management company (CMC) under a compliant GDPR framework
- Generate income for Dean and Ellis (who introduced the deal) through agreed referral or data licensing fees
- Ensure Philip Harrison benefits as the **anchor claimant** — the primary victim whose case proves the pattern

This is entirely separate from your personal case but built on the same evidence base.

### The Big Question Dean is Still Working to Resolve: The Annual Modelo 210s

This is one of the most important open questions in your case, and it's connected directly to Wincham's negligence.

**What is a Modelo 210?**
A Modelo 210 is Spain's annual tax return for non-residents who hold property in Spain. It is filed with AEAT, the Spanish tax authority.

**What most people assume:**
Many clients — and apparently Wincham themselves in their marketing — treat the Modelo 210 as something that only gets filed *when you sell* the property (to account for the Capital Gains Tax on the sale).

**What Spanish law actually requires:**
Under Spanish tax law, **non-resident property owners must file an annual Modelo 210 every year** — even if there is no income from the property and even if the property is simply being held. This applies whether you are a UK individual or a UK limited company owning a Spanish property. The form declares the imputed (deemed) rental income — essentially, Spain says "even if you're not renting it out, you *could* be, so we're going to tax you on a notional amount."

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **The critical question:** Did Wincham file annual Modelo 210 returns for Los Romeros Limited every year since 2009? We do not yet have definitive confirmation that they did. The Wincham portal data capture is helping to establish this.

### 🇬🇧 UNITED KINGDOM — Laws Broken

#### 1. Financial Services and Markets Act 2000 (FSMA 2000) — Section 19 & Section 24
**What it says:** You cannot carry on a regulated financial activity in the UK without FCA authorisation. Falsely claiming to be FCA-regulated is a criminal offence.
**What Wincham did:** Falsely claimed FCA regulatory number **FRN 615817** in 2014. This number does not exist on the FCA register. This is potentially a **criminal offence** under s.24 FSMA 2000, carrying up to **2 years' imprisonment and/or an unlimited fine**.

#### 2. Financial Services and Markets Act 2000 — Section 21 (Financial Promotion)
**What it says:** You cannot communicate financial promotions to the public without FCA approval or being an authorised firm.
**What Wincham did:** Wincham's entire marketing operation — website, brochures, videos, ITP savings claims — constitutes financial promotion. Without an authorised approver, every marketing communication was potentially unlawful. This also means the *contracts* made as a result of those promotions may be legally unenforceable.

#### 3. Money Laundering Regulations 2017 (MLR 2017) — Trust and Company Service Provider (TCSP) Registration
**What it says:** Any business providing Trust or Company Services — including company formation, registered office provision, nominee director and secretary services, and accounting — must register with HMRC as a TCSP and pass a "Fit and Proper" test.
**What Wincham did:** Ran 782 client shell companies as nominee director/secretary/registered office provider for over a decade. **No HMRC TCSP registration confirmed** for any Wincham entity. This is a **criminal offence** under Regulation 86 of MLR 2017, carrying an **unlimited fine and/or 2 years' imprisonment**.

#### 4. Companies Act 2006 — Sections 172–175 (Directors' Duties) and Section 174
**What it says:** A company director must act in the company's best interests, exercise reasonable care and skill, and avoid conflicts of interest. Section 174 specifically requires "reasonable care, skill and diligence."
**What Wincham did:** Mark Damion Roach is simultaneously director of **431 client companies**. It is structurally and physically impossible to discharge the directors' duties required by ss.172–175 across 431 companies simultaneously. Each appointment without proper oversight is a breach. Leonard Jones as director of the entity he beneficially owns is a conflict of interest breach under s.175.

#### 5. Companies Act 2006 — Section 853E (Confirmation Statements — SIC Codes)
**What it says:** Every company must file accurate SIC (Standard Industrial Classification) codes on its confirmation statement.
**What Wincham did:** Forensic analysis of Companies House data found that **399 out of 752 Wincham-administered companies** were mis-classified as SIC 70229 (Management Consultancy) when they were actually holding Spanish property — which should have been coded as real estate (SIC 68100). This mis-classification potentially concealed ATED (Annual Tax on Enveloped Dwellings) liability from HMRC. Each incorrect confirmation statement is a separate criminal offence.

#### 6. Companies Act 2006 — Section 790W (Delayed PSC Filing)
**What it says:** Changes to Persons with Significant Control (PSC) must be filed at Companies House promptly.
**What Wincham did:** Malcolm Roach's exit as PSC and James Roach's insertion were filed with a **20-month delay.** This is a criminal offence.

#### 7. Consumer Protection from Unfair Trading Regulations 2008
**What it says:** Businesses cannot use misleading commercial practices — including false claims about savings, benefits, or the nature of their services.
**What Wincham did:** Wincham consistently marketed their service by claiming it would save clients **"16.5% on Spanish property taxes (ITP)"** and that the UK company structure was a legitimate tax planning tool. Evidence from Spanish tax law suggests **AEAT does not accept this characterisation** — the ITP exemption they claimed does not apply in the way they described. This amounts to a false and/or misleading commercial representation.

#### 8. Property Misdescriptions Act 1991 / Consumer Rights Act 2015
**What it says:** Services must be provided as described, with reasonable care and skill.
**What Wincham did:** Failed to deliver the promised tax savings; failed to maintain compliant corporate structures; failed to maintain a business bank account for client companies; directed company money to personal accounts; failed to adapt their service post-Brexit.

#### 9. Data Protection Act 2018 / UK GDPR — ICO Registration & Data Handling
**What it says:** Any organisation holding personal data about 1,564+ individuals must be registered with the ICO and must process data lawfully.
**What Wincham did:** Has operated a database of over 1,500 people's financial and personal information. ICO registration not specifically confirmed. Potential data handling irregularities in how information was transferred between Wincham Group entities.

### 🇪🇸 SPAIN — Laws Broken (Spanish National Law)

#### 11. Ley 58/2003 General Tributaria — Article 47 (Mandatory Fiscal Representative)
**Plain English:** Spanish law says that non-residents using Spanish tax services must appoint a *representative who actually lives in Spain*. Wincham held itself out as your fiscal representative — but Wincham is a UK company with no confirmed Spanish residency. **They were the wrongdoer pretending to be the solution.**
**After Brexit (1 Jan 2021):** This became even more critical. The UK is now treated as a "third country" by Spain. Every Modelo 210 filing Wincham made for UK clients after 1 January 2021 may have been made by an entity that had **no legal authority to act**.
**Penalty per client, per year:** €2,000–€6,000 for absence of a compliant fiscal representative.

#### 12. Real Decreto Legislativo 5/2004 (IRNR) — Article 10 (Non-Resident Income Tax)
**Plain English:** Non-residents from countries outside the EU (including the UK after Brexit) must have a *properly qualified Spanish resident* as their fiscal representative. Mark Roach (a UK resident) running the Spanish company alone, after Ivars David left in 2016, means Wincham may have had **no qualified person at all** filing your Modelo 210 returns after 2016 — and certainly none eligible post-Brexit (from 2021).

#### 13. Ley 17/2009 "Ley Paraguas" (Services Directive Transposition) — Fiscal Services Exclusion
**Plain English:** EU law allows companies from one country to briefly offer services in another. But **Spanish law specifically carves out tax and fiscal services from this freedom.** Wincham cannot use "we were just an EU firm working across borders" as a defence — the law explicitly says that defence **does not apply to tax advice**.

#### 14. Colegio de Gestores Administrativos — Professional Monopoly
**Plain English:** In Spain, if you want to file tax returns on behalf of someone else before AEAT (the Spanish tax authority), you must be a registered *Gestor Administrativo* with the relevant professional college (Colegio). In Lanzarote, this is the **Colegio de Gestores Administrativos de Las Palmas**. Wincham is not registered with them. **They were not legally permitted to file your Spanish tax returns.**

#### 15. Cierre de Hoja Registral — Wincham International Spain SL
**Plain English:** One of Wincham's Spanish companies (*Wincham International Spain SL*) has a **closed registration sheet** at the Spanish commercial registry. This means it legally **cannot perform any legal acts** — it cannot sign contracts, file documents, or take any official action — until the closure is lifted. This company is legally inert.

**Plain English:** If Wincham acted as rental manager for any Lanzarote villa, they needed a Vivienda Vacacional (holiday rental) licence from the Cabildo de Lanzarote. No evidence of any such licence has been found.
