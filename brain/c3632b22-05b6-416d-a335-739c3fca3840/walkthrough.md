## 🔍 Research Synthesis: The Wincham Scheme

Based on the forensic analysis of all sources, here is the consolidated summary of the Wincham "Spanish Property" scheme:

### 1. The 'Wincham Scheme'
The strategy involved placing Spanish properties into UK Limited Company "shells" to supposedly avoid Spanish Inheritance Tax (IHT). Wincham marketed this to over **2,000 British retirees**, charging hefty annual fees for "administration" while the legal structure was a ticking time bomb.

### 2. The Negligence Cascade
*   **Brexit Failure:** After Jan 1, 2021, these UK companies lost their EU tax protections. Wincham failed to advise clients to exit, leading to massive tax "traps" upon property sales.
*   **Regulatory Deceit:** The group used a regulated firm (**Adrem Accounting**) as a front while clients actually dealt with unregulated entities.
*   **Conflicts of Interest:** Key administrators held secret ownership stakes in the companies they were meant to be independently overseeing.
*   **Regulatory Breaches:** The group operated without mandatory Anti-Money Laundering (AML) registration and claimed a fraudulent FCA number in marketing.

### 3. Impact on Victims
*   **Financial Ruin:** Sale proceeds are often blocked because no corporate bank accounts exist. Victims face a ~40% "phantom gain" tax charge.
*   **Hidden Liabilities:** Years of unfiled "Modelo 210" returns have created significant debt to the Spanish Hacienda (AEAT).
