Section 01

Visual Philosophy — Forensic Minimalism

Evidence-Led Layout

Every section follows the structure of a legal submission: high-severity finding first, supporting analysis second, citation last. Visual hierarchy must mirror informational hierarchy.

Structured Negative Space

Large sections of dark background separate evidence blocks, allowing regulatory data to breathe. White space is a deliberate evidence envelope, not an absence of content.

Controlled Colour

Colour is reserved exclusively for severity annotation and factual emphasis. No decorative colour. No random use of the brand gradient outside hero H1 headings.

Design Principles — Non-Negotiable
01 Clarity over decoration. If a visual element serves no evidential, structural, or navigational purpose, it does not exist.
02 Severity is semantic. Crimson means high. Amber means medium. Blue means informational. This is never reversed.
03 Data is dominant. Financial figures, statute references, and percentages are always rendered in font-weight:700, minimum 16px.
04 Structure is argument. The visual order of elements is a logical chain. Never break the hierarchy.
Section 02

Severity Classification System

Severity: HIGH

Critical — Immediate Action

Used for breaches of statutory duty, criminal exposure, outstanding regulatory non-compliance, or financial loss directly causable by professional negligence.

Example: Article 108 LMV anti-avoidance breach. Potential criminal liability.
Severity: MEDIUM

Significant — Follow-Up Required

Applied to outstanding tax returns, pending compliance actions, or systemic advisory omissions. Liability likely but not yet formally quantified.

Example: Modelo 210 annual returns unfiled for 15 years. Surcharges probable.
Informational

Background — Contextual

Supporting statutory definitions, jurisdiction context, timeline referencing, and named legal authority definitions that frame the higher-severity findings.

Example: Hedley Byrne principle — duty of care in professional adviser relationships.

Animation Rules — Non-Negotiable Constraints
✕ No scroll animations or parallax on legal evidence sections. Regulatory content must be visible immediately on scroll without motion dependency.
→ Smooth scroll only via CSS scroll-behavior: smooth for anchor navigation within page.
✕ No auto-playing pulsing or blinking on any severity badge or element. Static is authoritative. Motion is distraction.
→ Respect prefers-reduced-motion. All transitions must be gated behind this media query for accessibility compliance.
Section 06

Iconography Standard

Approved

SVG Lucide / Heroicons

All icons must be inline SVG from the Lucide or Heroicons library, 24Ã—24 viewBox, rendered at w-4 h-4 to w-6 h-6 context-dependent. Stroke-based, not filled.

Prohibited

Emoji as Decoration

Emoji characters are strictly prohibited as UI elements, icons, or decorative elements. They undermine the authoritative and formal character of the brand presentation.

Restricted

Brand Mark Usage

The Los Romeros crest/seal may appear in the navigation bar and footer only. It must never be used decoratively within body content, or scaled beyond 80px in any context.

Icon Colour Application Rules
Muted (#64748B): Navigation, structural elements, non-critical UI chrome
Crimson (#DC2626): High severity warnings, critical violation markers only
Amber (#D97706): Notice-level content, pending compliance actions
Blue (#2563EB): External regulatory portal links and informational references
