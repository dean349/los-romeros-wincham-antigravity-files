Section 01
ES / UK

Brand Positioning & Story

One-Liner

"Delivering unyielding clarity and forensic accountability across cross-border legal compliance."

Primary Objective

To document, pursue, and rectify instances of professional breach of duty — focusing sharply on undisclosed risks such as Article 108 LMV and miscalculated corporate exit tax cascades arising from negligent UK—Spain property structuring advice.

What The Brand Does

Los Romeros Limited acts as a corporate entity anchored in transparent, evidence-based reasoning. Initially structured as a property holding vehicle spanning UK and Spanish jurisdictions, the brand now stands as a vehicle for regulatory scrutiny, legal exposure analysis, and rigorous financial audit against substandard professional advice received in connection with the Lanzarote estate sale and subsequent UK tax cascades.

Positioning Strategy

The brand positions itself not as a commercial service trying to sell, but as an authoritative dossier presenting irrefutable facts. It occupies the shelf of legal compliance, forensic accounting, and regulatory enforcement — not marketing.

Section 02

Tone of Voice

Non-Negotiable

Serious & Resolute

There is no room for ambiguity or softening of language. Tone must convey the gravity of legal and financial exposure. Claims must be direct, avoiding hyperbolic marketing speak entirely.

Required

Factual & Evidentiary

Every statement is treated as a fact backed by a verifiable source. We do not use "we think" or "maybe." We use "forensic analysis demonstrates," "the statute dictates," and "the liability stands at."

Required

Precise & Structural

Information must be layered logically, mirroring legal briefs or forensic accounting reports. Use clear headings, numbered bullet points, and high-level summaries leading into granular detail.

Enforced

Unyielding & Transparent

Complete, unredacted financial truths over opaque assurances. The brand does not hedge. It does not apologise. It documents, classifies, and presents without editorialisation.

Personality Trait Summary
Authoritative

Commands respect through precision and citation

Forensic

Every claim has traceability and a source

Unyielding

Does not retract, soften, or qualify without new evidence

Transparent

All financial and legal data presented unredacted

Section 03

Messaging Framework

Theme 1

Regulatory Accountability

Professional networks must be held to statutory standards. Negligence has quantifiable consequences — not abstract injury.

Theme 2

Cross-Border Transparency

Legal structures spanning UK and Spain require granular, disclosed oversight of both jurisdictions' anti-avoidance laws — no gaps, no omissions.

Theme 3

Evidence-Based Disclosure

Complete, unredacted financial truths over opaque "16% saving" assurances. Every figure cited is independently verifiable.

Primary Brand Pitch / Objective

To document, pursue, and rectify instances of professional breach of duty, focusing sharply on undisclosed risks — including Article 108 LMV and miscalculated corporate exit tax cascades — so that every professional obligation violation results in a proportionate, traceable, regulatory consequence.

Section 04

Audience & Language Guidelines

Because this brand addresses regulators (ICO, ICAEW, FCA, AEAT), legal counterparts, and forensic accountants, the language must align with professional statutory contexts at all times.

Use These Statutory-aligned vocabulary
  • Forensic analysis demonstrates—
  • Material statutory risk
  • Regulatory reporting obligations
  • Duty of care (Hedley Byrne, Caparo)
  • Civil / Regulatory / Criminal violations
  • Sede Electrónica (AEAT portal)
  • Liability exposure
  • Compliance gap
  • The statute dictates—
  • The liability stands at [specific £/€ figure]
Never Use Prohibited language patterns
  • "Synergy," "Holistic," or corporate buzzwords
  • Emotional appeals ("tragedy," "sad situation")
  • Vague estimations without specific amounts
  • "We think" or "maybe" or "possibly"
  • Softening qualifiers ("somewhat," "perhaps")
  • Passive voice constructions
  • Marketing hyperbole of any kind
  • "We believe" (use "the evidence establishes")
Section 05

Writing Mechanics & Formatting

Active Voice — Mandatory

Correct

"Wincham failed to disclose the exit tax costs."

Never

"The exit tax costs were not disclosed by Wincham."

Legal Citation Format

Always italicise legal citations in full neutral citation format:

Hedley Byrne & Co Ltd v Heller & Partners Ltd [1964] AC 465

Structure for Density

Break dense information into scannable lists. Use bold text for regulatory bodies (ICAEW, AEAT, ICO) and statutes. Always lead with the highest-severity finding and descend in severity.
